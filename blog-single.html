What drives the organisation.

What drives Wazir Education Society? Have you ever wondered why we do what we do? What drives a person to do something for society with nothing in return?

Wazir Education Society started out humbly. We are still quite new. With our limited resources, we have been trying our best to make an impact on society. We organize classes regarding spoken English, mathematics and computers. We believe that these subjects are prime for children.

As per the UNESCO report of 2017-18, 35% of the world’s illiterate population resides in India. Some regions lack educational facilities whereas some give undue importance to education.The cost of sending the children to school is much higher than employing them. The neglect of education in this country is primarily because of the low standard of living and poverty. These people cannot afford to send their children to schools and, in fact, employ them either in the farmlands or in the informal sector. WES mostly covers the state of Madhya Pradesh for now. With it's headquarters in Shahdol, we are covering the major parts of the city.

Our primary goal is curbing this problem of illiteracy. We want the coming generation to have better lives. The children we teach are always so happy and motivated to learn, it's inspiring to see young minds thrive. We see so much potential everyday, it gives us all the pleasure to harvest these young minds.

So, the question was, what drives us? The smiles on these young ones. The innocent faces, that's what drives us. The thought of the future generation doing better than their predecessors, that's what has driven us all these months. We aren't philanthropists or donors, we are merely humans who couldn't bear the thought of such talented kids not harvesting their potential. You can do it too! Kindness always starts at home.

Here's what you can do to help the unprivileged kids around you:

  • Donate all your old books. NEVER throw them away.
  • Be kind to any child that comes to you for money.
  • You don'talways have to donate, but you can be kind.
  • Never be prejudicial to kids regardless of their race, colour, caste or sex.
  • Contact your nearest authority if you ever witness child abuse.
  • You can also donate your books/clothes to charity and non-government organisations.
There are so many things you can do. Be creative when it comes to helping, you never know how your one step can impact a child's life in the most positive way. At Wazir Education Society, we always encourage volunteers. Thank you for being a part of our story. We hope to get your support always. ❤️
